LATEST NEWS 

More than 1,800 explosions were observed by the SMM across the Donetsk region in the last week with ceasefire violations concentrated around Donetsk airport, south-west of Donetsk city, north-east of Donetsk city (Horlivka, Zaitseve and Svitlodarsk), and north-east of Mariupol (Kominternove and Oktiabr).



SMM monitors observed the use of restricted weapons across the line of contact, including multiple launch rocket systems, and noted their absence in storage sites and holding areas.



The SMM’s mandate was extended by one year to 31 March 2017 in a decision by the OSCE Permanent Council (see www.osce.org/cio/223236);

the SMM Chief Monitor Ertugrul Apakan welcomed the move, saying the Mission will continue to grow not only in numbers but also in technology and presence (read more:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/223406). 

From 15-17 February, the SMM Principal Deputy Chief Monitor Alexander Hug travelled to eastern Ukraine to assess the security situation and encourage parties to de-escalate, read more:

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/222901;

www.osce.org/ukraine-smm/222611. 

The Mission continued to experience freedom-of-movement restrictions across Donbas, mostly in areas not controlled by the Government.



The deteriorating general security and humanitarian situation in Donetsk region continues to put civilian lives at risk, and to cause damage to property and vital civilian infrastructure.
