FACTS MATTER 

Between 12 August and 25 August, the SMM recorded about 5,400 ceasefire violations, including about 1,100 explosions (compared with 2700 ceasefire violations, including over 600 explosions in the previous two-week period).



The SMM has recorded two civilian casualties:

an injury from the mine detonation on 3 July near Illienko and an injury from small-arm fire on 15 August in Zaitseve.

This brings the total number of civilian casualties since 1 January 2019, to 104 (15 deaths and 89 injuries).



The Mission observed fresh damage from the mortar fire to residential houses in Verkh- noshyrokivske and from small-arms fire to a school in Zolote-5/Mykhailivka.

For the fourth time this year, the SMM saw damage related to gunfire on buildings at a farm near government-controlled Novoluhanske.



The Mission observed 123 weapons in violation of withdrawal lines (111 in areas not controlled by the Government), as well as mines and unexploded ordnance.



The SMM continued to monitor the Stanytsia Luhanska disengagement area.

The Mis- sion observed activities related to the dismantling of former forward positions, as well as de-mining activities and clearing of vegetation close to the broken section of the bridge.



The Mission’s freedom of movement was restricted two times in government-controlled areas and 36 times in non-government-controlled areas, mostly in the southern part of Donetsk region.



SMM unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) were targeted by small-arm fire on eight occa- sions, and subject to signal interference, assessed by jamming, on 40 occasions.



The SMM continued facilitation of repairs and maintenance to essential civilian infra- structure, including the ongoing repairs to the Petrivske water pumping station near government-controlled Artema, which provides water to about 150,000 civilians.
